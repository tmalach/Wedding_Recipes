Penne al Baffo

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta
    • Bring a large pot of salted water (1 tablespoon salt for every 2 quarts of water) to a boil.
    • Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. Prepare the Sauce
    • In a large skillet, cook the bacon/guanciale/pancetta over medium heat. Drain the bacon/guanciale fat into a ramekin.
    • Add 2 tablespoons of bacon/guanciale fat to the pan and heat over medium heat. Add the finely chopped shallots and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    • Add garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
    • Add the white/sherry wine to deglaze the pan. Scrape off fond and let the liquid reduce to cook off alcohol.
  3. Add Tomato and Cream
    • Pour in the tomato passata and stir well to combine with the bacon/guanciale and shallots. Let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
    • Reduce the heat to a gentle simmer and stir in the heavy cream. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is well combined and creamy.
    • Reduce heat to low, and add the parmesan cheese and butter. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Combine Pasta and Sauce
    • Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the creamy tomato-bacon/guanciale sauce in batches, and toss everything together until the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce.
  5. Serve
    • Serve the Penne al Baffo immediately, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Tips

  • Quality Ingredients: Use high-quality tomato passata and meat for the best flavor. Fresh ingredients make a big difference in this simple dish. Many passata jars come in 24 ounces. If you are using the second half of the jar, and there is only 10 ounces left, reserve 1/4 cup of pasta water, add to the jar, shake, and add to the dish. This will collect all of the passata and add required volume.
  • Cream Consistency: Adjust the amount of cream to achieve your preferred sauce consistency. If you like it creamier, add a bit more cream.
  • Pasta Cooking: Make sure to cook the pasta al dente, as it will continue to cook slightly when tossed with the hot sauce.
  • Seasoning: Taste the sauce before adding salt, as the meat can be quite salty. Adjust the seasoning accordingly.
  • Herbs and Cheese: Fresh basil and Parmesan cheese add great flavor to the dish, but they are optional. Feel free to skip them if you prefer a simpler version.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the sauce ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat it gently on the stove before tossing with freshly cooked pasta.
