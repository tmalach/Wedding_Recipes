French Onion Chicken and Gnocchi

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Prepare Chicken and Gnocchi:
    • Pat chicken dry, and season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and thyme.
    • In a large oven-safe skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 4–5 minutes per side. Remove from the skillet and set aside. Chicken should be slightly undercooked to finish cooking in the oven with the gnocchi.
    • Let the gnocchi come to room temperature for about 15–20 minutes.
  2. Caramelize Onions:
    • Add butter and olive oil to the same skillet. Use the butter to scrape off bits of fond for extra flavor.
    • Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until deeply caramelized, about 30 minutes.
    • After 10–15 minutes of cooking onions, add a small amount of salt and sugar to aid caramelization. Deglaze with a splash of broth or wine if onions start to burn.
    • Add minced garlic and thyme in the last 2 minutes of cooking.
    • Preheat the oven to 425°F (190°C).
  3. Deglaze and Combine:
    • Deglaze the skillet with white wine, scraping up browned bits. Allow the wine to reduce by half, then add beef broth. Simmer for about 5 minutes.
    • Slice the chicken, then add the gnocchi and chicken to the skillet with onions.
    • Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15–20 minutes.
    • Remove from oven and top with cheeses. Broil for 2–3 minutes until cheese and gnocchi are lightly browned.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve the French onion chicken over a bed of crispy gnocchi. Garnish with additional fresh thyme if desired.

Tips

  • Brands to Consider: Look for gnocchi brands like De Cecco, Delallo, Rana, Racconto, or Caputo, often found in refrigerated or pasta aisles. La Pasta from Whole Foods also works well.
  • Enhancing Store-Bought Gnocchi: Sauté gnocchi in butter and olive oil after boiling for a golden, crispy exterior. Add minced garlic, herbs, or lemon juice while sautéing for extra flavor. Toss with freshly grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano before serving for richness.
  • Caramelizing Onions: Take your time to develop a deep, rich caramel color without burning for authentic flavor.
  • Deglazing: Use good-quality dry white wine to add acidity and complexity.
  • Broth Choice: Beef broth adds a rich, hearty flavor typical of French onion soup.
  • Cheese: Gruyère is traditional; Swiss or Emmental are good substitutes.
  • Chicken: Let chicken come to room temperature and pat dry before seasoning and cooking.
  • Texture: Optional: top with panko breadcrumbs for extra crunch.
