Garlic Hummus

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas:
    • Drain and rinse the chickpeas under cold water. If desired, remove the skins by gently rubbing them between your fingers. This step is optional but can help achieve a smoother texture.
  2. Blend Ingredients:
    • In a food processor, combine the tahini, lemon juice, ground cumin, ground chili powder, salt, and minced garlic. Process to blend flavors. Add olive oil and blend to process flavors again.
    • Add chickpeas to the mixture and process until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides of the food processor as needed.
    • Add ice cubes or ice water, 1 tablespoon/cube at a time, until you reach your desired consistency and creaminess. Blend for approximately 5 minutes with intermittent mixing to scrape hummus down from the sides.
  3. Adjust Seasoning:
    • Taste the hummus and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add more salt, garlic, or lemon juice according to your preference.
  4. Serve:
    • Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Drizzle with additional olive oil and sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley or a dash of paprika or sumac for garnish, if desired. Allowing the hummus to chill in the fridge for 30 minutes allows flavors to meld better.
  5. Enjoy:
    • Serve the garlic and olive oil hummus with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or use it as a spread or dip for sandwiches, wraps, or crackers.

Tips

  • Use Quality Ingredients: Start with high-quality chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, and fresh garlic. Fresh ingredients will contribute to a better flavor and texture. Soom, Joyva, and Krinos are all high quality tahini brands. Tahini should be stirred well before measuring to incorporate any separated oil.
  • Process Thoroughly: Process the ingredients in a food processor for several minutes until smooth and creamy. Be patient and scrape down the sides of the processor bowl as needed to ensure everything gets blended evenly.
  • Adjust Consistency: If the hummus is too thick, you can add water, a little at a time, until you reach the desired consistency. This will help achieve a smoother texture.
  • Taste and Adjust: Taste the hummus before serving and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can add more salt, garlic, lemon juice, or olive oil according to your taste preferences.
  • Experiment with Flavor: While this recipe calls for garlic and olive oil, feel free to customize the flavors to suit your preferences. You can add additional ingredients like roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, fresh herbs, or spices to create unique variations of hummus.
  • Serve with Accompaniments: Serve the hummus with your favorite accompaniments such as pita bread, fresh vegetables, crackers, or as a spread for sandwiches and wraps. You can also drizzle it with extra olive oil and sprinkle with herbs or spices for a decorative touch.
  • Store Properly: Store any leftover hummus in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will keep well for several days. Before serving, let it come to room temperature or give it a quick stir to restore its creamy texture. Drizzle a thin layer of olive oil on top to help preserve freshness.
  • Consistency: For an extra smooth texture, you can peel the garlic cloves before mincing them.
  • Flavor: Adjust the amount of garlic according to your taste preferences. If you prefer a stronger garlic flavor, you can add more cloves.
