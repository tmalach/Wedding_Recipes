Notes
Water Density The density of water should be about 1.0 g/ml.
Added Volume from Dissolved Sugar Dissolving the sugar in water should add about 0.6-0.65 ml per gram of sugar. However, during the simmering process, the volume may be smaller than the theoretical calculation due to evaporation.
Simple Syrup Density The density of the simple syrup mixture should be about 1.44 g/ml. Or 1.16
Everclear Density The density of the infused Everclear (75.5%) should be about 0.84-0.86 g/ml. If using 95% Everclear, the density should be about 0.8-0.82 g/ml. This recipe uses 0.83 g/ml for both.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lemons:
    • Wash the lemons thoroughly to remove any dirt or wax.
  2. Isolate the Rind from the Lemons:
    • Use a paring knife to peel off the rind from the lemons, avoiding the white pith as much as possible. Alternatively, use a zester or microplane to remove the lemon zest.
    • Shear or shave (preferred) off extra pith from the lemon rinds with the paring knife if necessary.
    • Place the lemon peels in a large glass jar or airtight container.
  3. Infuse the Everclear:
    • Pour the Everclear over the lemon peels, making sure they are fully submerged.
    • Seal the jar tightly and let it sit at room temperature in a dark spot for at least 20–30 (preferred) days. The peels can also sit for longer than 30 days. Mix intermittently.
  4. Make the Dilutant:
    • After the infusion period, prepare simple syrup by combining the water and sugar in a saucepan over medium heat.
    • Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved. The mixture should come to a gentle simmer but do not boil. Stir often/constantly.
    • Once fully dissolved, remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Make the Limoncello and Chill:
    • Strain the infused liquor through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the lemon peels. Discard the peels.
    • Gradually add the infused liquor to the cooled simple syrup while mixing.
    • Seal the bottles and store them in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours before serving. Ideally, let them steep for 2+ weeks for flavors to infuse and mellow. Alternatively, the Limoncello can also mellow in a cool dark place instead.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve the Limoncello chilled in small glasses as a refreshing digestif. Store in refrigerator long-term. The Limoncello should be approximately 34% ABV. This recipe makes 1000 ml of Limoncello.

Tips

  • Choose organic lemons: Since you'll be using the peel/zest of the lemons, it's best to use organic ones to avoid any pesticides or wax on the peel.
  • Infusion time: The longer you let the lemon peels infuse in the Everclear, the stronger the lemon flavor will be. While a minimum of 1 week is recommended, you can let it infuse for 6+ weeks for a more intense flavor.
  • Straining: When straining the infused Everclear, use a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any lemon particles or sediment, resulting in a smooth Limoncello.
  • Adjust sweetness: Taste the Limoncello as you add the simple syrup to adjust the sweetness to your liking. Some prefer it sweeter, while others prefer it less sweet. Start with less syrup and gradually add more until you reach your desired sweetness level. If altering the Brix of the Limoncello, be aware that the ABV will be altered.
  • Chilling: After bottling, refrigerate the Limoncello for at least 4 hours before serving. Serving it chilled enhances the refreshing taste. Allowing the Limoncello to steep for an additional 1–2+ weeks will mellow flavor and make a smoother product.
  • Presentation: Consider bottling the Limoncello in decorative glass bottles or jars for a stylish presentation, especially if you plan to give it as a gift.
  • Enjoy responsibly: Limoncello is typically served in small glasses as a digestif after meals. It's potent, so enjoy it in moderation.
