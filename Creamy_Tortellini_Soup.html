Instructions

  1. Cook the Sausage:
    • Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add sausage and cook until browned and cooked through, about 3–5 minutes. Leave 2 tablespoons of fat behind.
  2. Cook the Vegetables:
    • Add the onion and sauté until the vegetables are softened, about 3–5 minutes.
  3. Bloom the Aromatics:
    • Stir in the garlic, Italian seasoning, paprika, fennel seed, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Cook for another minute until fragrant. Do not add too much salt at this point.
    • Add tomato paste and cook for 2–3 minutes until it starts to brown a bit.
  4. Add Chicken Stock and Passata:
    • Gradually pour in the chicken broth and tomato passata. Simmer 10 minutes.
  5. Cook the Tortellini:
    • Bring the soup to a simmer, then add the tortellini. Cook according to the package instructions, usually about 5–7 minutes, until the tortellini are tender. The tortellini can be a little bit under since it will continue to cook with the spinach.
  6. Cook the Spinach:
    • Stir in the chopped spinach and cook until wilted, about 2–3 minutes.
  7. Add Cream, Cheese, and Basil:
    • Add heavy cream and let the mixture simmer for 2–3 minutes.
    • Add the grated Parmesan cheese, stirring until melted and the soup is creamy.
    • Reduce the heat to low and add basil and lemon juice and adjust the seasoning with additional salt and pepper, if needed.
  8. Serve:
    • Serve the soup hot, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of fresh herbs if desired.

Tips

  • Sauté Vegetables Properly
    • Why: Sautéing the vegetables (onions, garlic, carrots, and celery) properly helps to build a flavorful base for the soup.
    • Tip: Make sure the vegetables are softened and just starting to caramelize to release their natural sweetness and enhance the overall flavor of the soup.
  • Use High-Quality Broth
    • Why: The broth forms the backbone of the soup’s flavor.
    • Tip: Use a high-quality chicken or vegetable broth. If you have homemade broth, it’s even better. Store-bought broth can be enhanced with a little bit of extra seasoning or by simmering it with additional herbs.
  • Add Dairy Gradually
    • Why: Adding cream or half-and-half gradually prevents it from curdling.
    • Tip: Lower the heat before adding the cream and stir continuously. You can temper the cream by adding a small amount of hot broth to it before mixing it into the pot.
  • Cook Tortellini to Al Dente
    • Why: Overcooked tortellini can become mushy.
    • Tip: Cook the tortellini just until al dente, following the package instructions. They will continue to cook slightly in the hot soup.
  • Season to Taste
    • Why: Proper seasoning is crucial for a balanced flavor.
    • Tip: Taste the soup before serving and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. A bit of freshly grated nutmeg can also enhance the flavor of creamy soups.
  • Fresh Spinach at the End
    • Why: Adding spinach at the end ensures it remains vibrant and retains its nutrients.
    • Tip: Stir in the spinach just before serving and cook until just wilted. This keeps the spinach from becoming overcooked and slimy.
  • Cheese for Creaminess
    • Why: Parmesan cheese adds a rich, umami flavor and creaminess.
    • Tip: Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese and stir it in just before serving for a smooth, creamy texture.
  • Storage and Reheating
    • Why: Proper storage ensures the soup retains its quality.
    • Tip: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently over low heat to avoid breaking the creamy base. Add a splash of broth or cream if the soup thickens too much upon standing.
  • Optional Garnishes
    • Why: Garnishes add an extra layer of flavor and texture.
    • Tip: Garnish with extra Parmesan cheese, lemon zest, crushed red pepper, or fresh parsley for added flavor and a pop of color.
