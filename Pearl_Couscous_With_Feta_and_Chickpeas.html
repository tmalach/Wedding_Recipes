Pearl Couscous With Feta and Chickpeas

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Prep the Chicken
    • Preheat oven to 450°F (232°C).
    • Pound chicken breasts to 3–6 mm (⅛ to ¼ inch) thick for quick, even cooking.
    • Pat chicken dry with paper towels, then season generously with salt and black pepper.
    • Let chicken rest at room temperature for 30 minutes if time allows.
  2. Cook the Chicken
    • In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons (30 ml) olive oil over medium-high heat.
    • Cook chicken for 4–6 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
    • Transfer to a plate and keep warm. Work in batches if needed to avoid crowding the pan.
  3. Roast the Tomatoes
    • In a 3-quart (2.8 L) baking dish, combine cherry tomatoes, chopped chives, 2 tablespoons (30 ml) olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, ½ teaspoon salt, black pepper, and oregano.
    • Roast in preheated oven for 8–12 minutes, or until tomatoes are soft and begin to burst. Use the “potato test”: a knife should slide through with little resistance.
  4. Prepare the Stock Mixture
    • In the same skillet used for the chicken, pour in the chicken stock and bring to a boil.
    • Deglaze the pan by scraping up the browned bits. Stir in 1 teaspoon salt, fresh chopped cilantro, lemon juice, and cumin.
  5. Assemble and Bake the Casserole
    • Remove baking dish from the oven. Add couscous and chickpeas to the roasted tomatoes.
    • Pour the hot seasoned chicken stock over the mixture and stir gently.
    • Cover the dish tightly with foil and bake for 20 minutes.
  6. Add Chicken and Cheese
    • While the couscous bakes, slice the cooked chicken into strips.
    • After 20 minutes, remove the foil from the baking dish. Stir in ¾ of the feta, grated Parmesan, sliced chicken, and olives.
    • Return to the oven and bake uncovered for 5–10 minutes, or until the cheese is soft and melty.
  7. Serve
    • Serve warm, topped with the remaining feta, additional herbs (such as parsley or basil), or more Parmesan if desired.

Tips

  • Feta Quality: Use high-quality feta stored in brine for the best flavor and creaminess when melted.
  • Couscous Type: Pearl couscous is also labeled as Israeli or Palestinian couscous — all work well in this dish.
  • Broth Substitution: Use vegetable or chicken broth instead of water to deepen the flavor of the couscous.
  • Protein Swap: Shrimp can be used instead of chicken for a lighter protein. Add it during the last 10 minutes of baking.
  • Grain Alternative: Orzo can be substituted for couscous. Add an additional 5 minutes to the baking time if using.
  • Avoid Overcrowding: Don’t overcrowd the baking dish; this ensures better browning and caramelization of the tomatoes and scallions.
  • Pre-Tossing: Toss vegetables with oil, vinegar, and seasonings before roasting to infuse flavor throughout.
  • High Heat Roasting: Roast at a high temperature (232°C / 450°F) for concentrated flavor and color.
  • Add Greens: Stir in kale, spinach, or Swiss chard for a boost of nutrition and texture.
  • Boiling Broth First: Bring the broth to a full boil before adding it to the baking dish for better heat distribution.
  • Steam Tightly: Cover the dish tightly with foil to ensure the couscous steams properly and cooks evenly.
  • Fluffing: Fluff the couscous with a fork after baking to separate grains and prevent clumping.
  • Fresh Feta Finish: Reserve a portion of the feta to crumble on top before serving for a fresh, tangy finish.
  • Melty Cheese Note: Parmesan or Asiago offer a salty, nutty layer of richness when stirred in during the final bake.
  • Resting Time: Let the dish rest for 5 minutes after baking so the cheese melts evenly and flavors settle.
  • Herb Garnish: Sprinkle with fresh parsley, cilantro, or mint for a burst of color and herbal brightness.
  • Final Touch: A drizzle of quality olive oil and balsamic vinegar adds depth and richness just before serving. Finely grated lemon zest adds a subtle citrus flavor and brightens up the dish. Use a microplane grater to zest the lemon for the best results.
  • Best Temperature: Serve warm while the feta is still soft and creamy for the best texture and flavor.
