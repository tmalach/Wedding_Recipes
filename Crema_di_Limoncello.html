Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta
    • Bring a large pot of salted water (1 tablespoon salt for every 2 quarts of water) to a boil.
    • Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
    • If using the last half of the passata bottle, reserve 1/4 cup of pasta water.
    • Drain the pasta and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce
    • In a large skillet, cook the bacon/guanciale/pancetta over medium-high heat. Drain the bacon/guanciale fat into a ramekin.
    • Add 2 tablespoons of bacon/guanciale fat to the pan and heat over medium heat. Add the finely chopped shallots and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    • Add garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
    • Add the white/sherry wine to deglaze the pan. Scrape off fond and let reduce/cook off alcohol.
  3. Add Tomato and Cream
    • Pour in the tomato passata and stir well to combine with the bacon/guanciale and shallots. Let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
    • Pour 1/4 cup of pasta water into the tomato passata jar, shake, and add to the pot if using the last half of the bottle.
    • Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is well combined and creamy.
    • Add the parmesan cheese and butter, and season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Combine Pasta and Sauce
    • Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the creamy tomato-bacon/guanciale sauce, and toss everything together until the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce.
    • Add the pasta in batches to achieve desired sauciness level. 12 ounces of pasta works well for the sauce amount.
  5. Serve
    • Serve the Penne al Baffo immediately, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
