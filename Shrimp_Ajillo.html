Instructions

  1. Cook the Rice:
    • Cook the yellow rice according to package instructions. Once done, fluff with a fork and set aside, covered to keep warm.
  2. Infuse the Oil with Garlic and Cayenne:
    • While the rice is cooking, add the olive oil and cayenne pepper to a large cold skillet. Stir to distribute the cayenne evenly in the oil.
    • Once the rice is ready, turn the skillet heat to high. Add a few pieces of garlic and wait until they begin to sizzle—this is your sign the oil is hot enough.
    • Immediately reduce the heat to medium and stir in the remaining garlic. Cook gently, stirring often, until the garlic becomes light golden and fragrant, about 2–3 minutes. Avoid browning too much or the garlic may become bitter.
  3. Cook the Shrimp:
    • Increase the heat back to high and add the shrimp to the skillet. Stir continuously to coat the shrimp in the garlic oil and distribute the heat evenly.
    • Cook until the shrimp are pink and opaque, about 3–4 minutes. Do not overcook — once they curl and turn bright pink, they’re done.
    • Serve the garlic shrimp over the warm yellow rice.

Tips

  • Shrimp Size: Medium shrimp (41–50 count per pound) cook quickly. Keep an eye on them — overcooked shrimp become rubbery.
  • Garlic Prep: For best flavor and texture, mince garlic finely by hand rather than using a garlic press, which can make it too wet and bitter.
  • Infusing Oil: Always start with cold oil when infusing with spices or aromatics like garlic and cayenne. It allows the flavors to develop gently and prevents scorching.
  • Spice Level: ¾ teaspoon cayenne gives noticeable heat. Adjust up or down to taste, or substitute with red pepper flakes for a different flavor profile.
  • Rice Timing: Starting the oil infusion while the rice cooks keeps things efficient — the shrimp only take a few minutes once the rice is done.
  • Make it a Meal: Add steamed broccoli, peas, or sautéed spinach on the side for a balanced dinner.
