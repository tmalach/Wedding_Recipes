White Bean Salad With Crispy Cheese

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

  • For the Salad
  • For the Dressing
  • For the Crispy Cheese

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crispy Cheese:
    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
    • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    • Spread the grated Parmesan cheese in small mounds (about 2 tablespoons each) on the parchment paper, flattening them slightly.
    • Bake for about 5–7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and golden brown. Let them cool completely on the baking sheet; they will become crispy as they cool.
  2. Make the Dressing:
    • In a small bowl or mason jar, whisk or shake together the olive oil, mustard, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, minced garlic, red pepper flakes, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  3. Assemble the Salad:
    • In a large bowl, combine the white beans, cherry tomatoes, arugula, red onion, cucumber, carrots, mozzarella, parsley, and basil.
    • Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve:
    • Top the salad with the crispy cheese rounds just before serving to maintain their crispiness.

Tips

  • Fresh Ingredients: Use the freshest possible vegetables for the best flavor and texture. Ripe cherry tomatoes and crisp cucumbers will enhance the overall taste.
  • Rinse the Beans: If using canned beans, rinse them thoroughly to remove any excess sodium and to improve their flavor.
  • Uniform Cuts: Try to cut the vegetables uniformly for an aesthetically pleasing presentation and even texture in every bite.
  • Balance Flavors: Taste the dressing before adding it to the salad. Adjust the seasoning by adding more lemon juice for acidity, olive oil for richness, or salt and pepper as needed.
  • Emulsify Well: Whisk the dressing ingredients well to ensure they are fully combined and emulsified. This will help coat the salad evenly.
  • Make Extra: Make extra dressing in a mason jar to adjust the amount of dressing used as needed.
  • Let Cheese Cool: Allow the cheese to cool completely on the parchment paper to achieve the desired crispiness. Use a spatula to carefully remove them once cooled.
  • Storage: The salad can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Refresh the salad with a little extra lemon juice and olive oil if needed before serving.
