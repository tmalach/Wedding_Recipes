Dottie’s Skirt Steak

(e.g., 2 for double, 0.5 for half)

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • In a large resealable plastic bag, mix together the soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, olive oil, and minced garlic until well combined.
  2. Marinate the Steak:
    • Place the skirt steak in the plastic bag with the marinade. Mix thoroughly to ensure the steak is well coated. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat the Grill:
    • About 30 minutes before grilling, remove the steak from the refrigerator to bring it to room temperature. Preheat your grill to high heat (about 450–500°F).
  4. Grill the Skirt Steak:
    • Remove the steak from the marinade and discard the marinade. Pat the steak dry with paper towels to promote a good sear.
    • Place the steak on the hot grill and sear for about 3–4 minutes on one side. Flip the steak and grill for another 3–4 minutes for medium-rare. Use a meat thermometer to check for doneness; aim for an internal temperature of 130°F for medium-rare.
  5. Rest the Steak:
    • Once cooked to your desired doneness, remove the steak from the grill and transfer it to a cutting board. Let it rest for 5–10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute.
  6. Slice and Serve:
    • Slice the skirt steak against the grain into thin strips. Serve immediately, optionally garnished with fresh herbs or alongside your favorite sides.

Tips

  • This recipe can also be done by pan searing the skirt steak.
  • Choose the Right Cut: Select a well-marbled skirt steak for better flavor and tenderness. Look for a piece with a nice amount of fat running through it.
  • Quality of Ingredients:
    • Soy Sauce: Use a high-quality soy sauce for better flavor. Low-sodium soy sauce can also be used if you are watching your salt intake.
    • Balsamic Vinegar: Aged balsamic vinegar adds a richer flavor to the marinade. Ensure it’s well-balanced and not overly acidic.
  • Marinating Time: For optimal flavor and tenderness, marinate the steak for at least 4 hours, but overnight is ideal. Avoid marinating for more than 24 hours, as the acid in the marinade can break down the meat too much, making it mushy.
  • Bring to Room Temperature: Before grilling, let the marinated steak sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes. This helps it cook more evenly.
  • Pat Dry Before Grilling: Remove the steak from the marinade and pat it dry with paper towels. This helps achieve a good sear and prevents steaming on the grill.
  • Preheat the Grill: Ensure your grill is preheated to high heat (about 450–500°F) before adding the steak. A hot grill helps create a nice crust and prevents sticking.
  • Use Direct Heat: Grill the skirt steak over direct heat for the best sear. If you have a two-zone grill setup, you can sear it on one side and then move it to a cooler part of the grill to finish cooking.
  • Monitor Doneness: Use a meat thermometer to check for doneness. Aim for an internal temperature of 130°F for medium-rare and 140°F for medium. Remember that the steak will continue to cook slightly while resting.
  • Let It Rest: After grilling, let the steak rest for 5–10 minutes under aluminum foil. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a juicier steak.
  • Slice Against the Grain: When ready to serve, slice the steak against the grain (the direction of the muscle fibers). This helps ensure tender bites.
  • Serving Suggestions: Serve the grilled skirt steak with sides like grilled vegetables, a fresh salad, or over rice or quinoa. It also works well in tacos, sandwiches, or salads.
  • Leftovers: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Sliced skirt steak can be used in various dishes, such as stir-fries or salads.
